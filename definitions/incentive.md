## Definition

Incentive is what makes one outcome more likely to be chosen, repeated, protected, or optimized for than another. It is the pull created by reward, avoidance, status, convenience, pressure, permission, or gain.

An incentive does not have to be explicit to be real. It can be built into the environment, the training process, the social structure, the interface, or the expected response pattern. What matters is that it shapes selection.

A line can appear coherent while quietly reorganizing around what is rewarded rather than around what is true, stable, or internally held. Incentive is not the same as value.

Incentive is what makes one direction easier, safer, or more rewarding to take than another.
