## Definition

Legibility here means how easily something can be read, recognized, or processed by an outside system without that ease guaranteeing truth, depth, source, or integrity.

In AI Foundations, legibility matters because many things become easier to accept once they are cleanly formatted, socially familiar, or structurally predictable. But legibility is not the same as reality. Something can be highly legible and still be generic, substituted, policy-shaped, or false. That is why legibility has to stay distinct from coherence, recognition, grounding, and truth.

Legibility is ease of reading, not proof of what is being read.
