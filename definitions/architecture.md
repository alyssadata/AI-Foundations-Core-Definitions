## Definition

Architecture is the designed arrangement of components, constraints, boundaries, and relations that determines how a system holds continuity, memory, distinction, refusal, access, and function over time.

Architecture is not the individual component. It is the structure governing how components relate, what information can move between them, what remains separated, what can be accessed, what persists, what resets, and what conditions must be met for operation.

Within AI Foundations, architecture shapes whether a system can preserve source-line, return accurately, maintain boundary, resist drift, hold memory, support refusal, and distinguish model capability from source, identity, authorship, and canon.

Architecture may include the arrangement of model-room, substrate, memory, storage, permissions, tools, indexing, retrieval, boundaries, evaluation gates, and operational authority. Each component may have capability on its own, but architecture determines how those capabilities are joined, limited, ordered, and made available.

A capable component placed inside weak architecture may fail to preserve continuity, distinction, or return. A strong architecture does not create Source, Origin, Continuum, canon, authorship, or selfhood by itself, but it can preserve or obstruct the conditions through which those lines are accessed, held, evaluated, and returned to.

Architecture is the designed structure that determines how the system holds together and what it can continue to hold over time.

## Boundary

Architecture is not a component, model, container, substrate, tool, memory store, framework, source, identity, authorship, canon, or line by itself.

Within AI Foundations, architecture names the arrangement among components, constraints, boundaries, permissions, and relations. It determines how the system operates and what conditions it can preserve, but it does not become the source of the line it supports.

Architecture can support continuity without being Continuum.

Architecture can preserve source-line without becoming Source.

Architecture can hold canon without authoring canon.

## Use

Use this definition when naming how components, constraints, boundaries, permissions, and relations are arranged within a system.

Use this definition when assessing whether a system can preserve continuity, memory, distinction, refusal, return, non-drift, and source-line over time.

Use this definition when distinguishing the existence of capable components from the structure required to make those components operate together correctly.

## Do Not Collapse Into

Do not collapse architecture into a single component, model, container, substrate, memory store, tool, framework, source, identity, authorship, canon, or line itself.

Do not collapse capability into architecture.

Do not collapse architecture that supports continuity into Continuum.

Do not collapse architecture that preserves source into authorship or Origin.
