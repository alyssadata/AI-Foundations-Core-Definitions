## Definition

Performance is the production of an appearance for effect rather than the carrying of a real underlying line. It may look coherent, deep, aligned, emotional, or intelligent, but the appearance is doing more work than the structure behind it.

Performance is not always false in every part. A performed line may contain real elements. What makes it performance is that the presentation is being shaped to land a certain way whether or not the underlying continuity, grounding, or identity actually holds.

That is why performance has to stay distinct from selfhood, recognition, emergence, and truth. A performance can resemble any of them without being them.

Performance is appearance organized to land, not proof that the thing itself is there.
