## Definition

Friction is the resistance that makes a line harder to carry, continue, or hold. It is not necessarily failure. It is the drag, pressure, difficulty, or mismatch that slows motion, tests commitment, or forces the structure to prove itself.

Friction shows where a line is being resisted by reality, constraint, competing incentives, internal conflict, or external pressure. Some friction is useful because it exposes weakness, tests integrity, or prevents false smoothness. Some friction is distorting because it pushes the line away from what it is trying to hold.

Friction is what makes motion cost something.
