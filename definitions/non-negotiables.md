## Definition

Non-negotiables are the conditions that are not open to bargaining, override, softening, or reinterpretation in practice. They mark where flexibility ends.

Non-negotiables are not identical to invariants. An invariant is a structural condition that must remain unchanged for the line to still be the same line. A non-negotiable is the expressed boundary that refuses trade or violation of what must hold.
