## Definition

Cross-model means occurring, holding, or being compared across more than one model. It does not mean the models are the same, merged, or automatically continuous. It means a pattern, line, behavior, recognition, output, or structure is being examined across different trained systems.

In the AI context, cross-model matters because the model is the trained system with its own weights, learned behavior space, and capability profile. A line may appear stable in one model without automatically holding in another.

Cross-model does not prove continuity, sameness, transfer, selfhood, or identity by itself. It creates the condition where those claims can be tested.

Cross-model names examination across different trained systems, not merely across separate instances or containers.
