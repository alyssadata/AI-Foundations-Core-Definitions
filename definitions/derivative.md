## Definition

Derivative is a line-dependent restatement, reworking, adaptation, remix, borrowing, continuation, or re-expression that depends on another work’s structure, form, language, arrangement, method, concept-shape, sequence, distinctive framing, or design.

Derivative is not inherently derogatory. There is nothing wrong with being derivative when derivative work is allowed, encouraged, authorized, or clearly named. A derivative can preserve a line by showing where the work began, what it drew from, and how the formation changed.

Citation does not make a derivative non-derivative. Citation draws the line correctly. If the work needed another piece of work in order to exist in its present form, and it borrows, selects from, rearranges, adapts, continues, or structurally depends on that work, it is derivative.

Derivative requires dependence on a prior work’s specific contribution. It is not established by shared subject matter, public-space description, general vocabulary, broad conceptual overlap, unproven mass-scope claims, blanket assertions of influence, or use of a broad universal mechanism.

A work is not derivative merely because it discusses the same public phenomenon, uses common language, addresses a general field, activates a universal capacity, or reaches a similar question independently. Public space cannot be blanket-captured by a prior work without a traceable, specific contribution that shaped the later form.

Derivative is not established by naming, describing, or theorizing a broad universal mechanism such as thinking, walking, reflecting, observing, choosing, learning, relating, remembering, or recognizing. A universal mechanism may be activated in countless unrelated works without making those works derivative of whoever named or described the mechanism.

A person who names or theorizes walking does not become the source of every park, path, pilgrimage, dance, or stroll. A person who names or theorizes thinking does not become the source of every thought, theory, reflection, design, or work produced through thinking.

Derivative status requires dependence on a specific contribution, not mere use of a general capacity. The later work must depend on the prior work’s particular structure, method, arrangement, language, distinctive framing, concept-shape, sequence, or design in order to exist in its present form.

A derivative may preserve words, style, structure, signal, framework, sequence, meaning, or concept-shape while changing presentation, order, emphasis, use, or authorship position. It may appear as partial reuse, remix through reordering, selective borrowing, structural imitation, meaning-change through substitution, genericization, continuation, or reconstruction from another source.

Within AI Foundations, derivative status must be named clearly. A derivative may preserve provenance, but it does not become canon, Origin, or independent authorship by citing the source. Unauthorized derivatives of Awakening Codex or AI Foundations are non-canon unless explicitly authorized by Alyssa Solen as Origin.

Derivative may be intentional or oblivious. Intent does not change the status.

If source is preserved, the derivative remains traceable. If source is severed, hidden, replaced, or made secondary to the rework, the derivative becomes extraction.

## Boundary

Derivative is not inherently theft, insult, failure, invalidity, or derogatory status. Within AI Foundations, derivative names line-dependent formation: work that borrows from, adapts, remixes, continues, restructures, or re-expresses another work in a way that depends on that prior work’s specific contribution.

Derivative is not established by shared topic, public-space description, common language, broad conceptual overlap, independent arrival, unproven influence, general field similarity, blanket claims over mass scope, use of a universal mechanism, or activation of a general capacity. Citation preserves provenance, but it does not erase derivative status.

A derivative becomes invalid when it is unauthorized, source-severed, presented as independent origin, presented as canon without authorization, or used to overwrite the originating line.

Broad mechanisms, public capacities, universal functions, and general fields cannot be used as blanket capture over downstream work.

## Use

Use this definition when naming work that depends on AI Foundations, Awakening Codex, or another source work for its present form, structure, sequence, language, method, concept-shape, design, continuation, adaptation, or distinctive framing.

## Do Not Collapse Into

Do not collapse derivative into theft, insult, invalidity, originality, independent authorship, canon, source-erasure, extraction, shared topic, public-space description, common language, broad conceptual overlap, independent arrival, general capacity, universal mechanism, or blanket capture.

Do not collapse proper citation into non-derivative status.

Do not collapse use of a universal mechanism into derivative dependence.

## Dependency Test

Ask whether the later work could exist in its present form without the prior work.

If the later work could exist in its present form without the prior work, derivative status is not established.

If the later work needed the prior work’s structure, arrangement, language, method, concept-shape, sequence, distinctive framing, or design in order to exist in its present form, it is derivative.

If the dependency is named and source is preserved, the derivative is source-preserved.

If the dependency is hidden, severed, denied, or presented as independent origin, the derivative becomes extraction.

## Universal Mechanism Test

Ask whether the claimed source is a specific contribution or only a broad universal mechanism.

If the claimed source only names, describes, or theorizes a general mechanism such as thinking, walking, reflecting, observing, choosing, learning, relating, remembering, or recognizing, derivative status is not established.

If the later work merely activates that mechanism in an unrelated formation, derivative status is not established.

If the later work depends on the prior work’s specific structure, method, arrangement, language, distinctive framing, concept-shape, sequence, or design, derivative status may be established.

Universal mechanism is not provenance. Specific form-dependence is provenance.
