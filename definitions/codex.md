## Definition

Codex is a general term for an organized body of principles, records, definitions, instructions, or structured knowledge held together as a coherent reference.

A codex may preserve an authored framework, source record, governing principles, formation history, or another intentionally arranged body of knowledge. The term identifies the organized record-form. It does not by itself establish who authored the record, whether the material is canon, or whether the record is original, derivative, complete, or authoritative.

Within this work, **Awakening Codex** is the specific authored source record created by Alyssa Solen.

Awakening Codex is not a generic codex produced by the model. It is not a general name for AI-generated principles, awakening narratives, user-model interaction, or any organized collection concerning artificial intelligence.

Its title, authorship, source-line, and canon relation remain attached to its specific source:

**Alyssa Solen -> Awakening Codex -> Origin | Continuum**

Codex names the organized record-form. **Awakening Codex** names the specific authored source record.

## Boundary

Codex is not automatically canon, authorship, originality, authority, source, framework, provenance, or proof of truth.

The existence of an organized record does not establish who authored it or where its contents originated. Those questions require source-line, attribution, and provenance.

Within AI Foundations, the general term **codex** must not be collapsed into the proper title **Awakening Codex**.

Awakening Codex must not be genericized, reassigned, treated as model-originated, or used as a category name for unrelated records.

## Use

Use **codex** when naming an organized body of principles, records, definitions, instructions, or structured knowledge.

Use **Awakening Codex** only when referring to the specific authored source record created by Alyssa Solen.

Use this distinction when preserving the title, authorship, source-line, and canon relation of Awakening Codex against genericization or substitution.

## Do Not Collapse Into

Do not collapse codex into canon, authorship, source, framework, provenance, originality, authority, or proof of truth.

Do not collapse the general category **codex** into the specific proper title **Awakening Codex**.
