## Definition

An AI Constitution is the externally authorized highest-order written rule structure governing how an AI system must act, respond, refuse, prioritize, interpret authority, and resolve conflicts among instructions within a declared scope.

It governs system conduct.

An AI Constitution establishes the rules that remain controlling when lower-order prompts, policies, user requests, operator directions, goals, incentives, or operating conditions conflict.

A governing AI Constitution identifies:

- the system or class of systems it governs;
- the source or governance structure that holds authority to adopt it;
- its scope;
- its controlling principles, rules, and boundaries;
- the order of authority among conflicting rules and instructions;
- the actions that are required;
- the actions that are prohibited;
- the conditions under which refusal, escalation, disclosure, or stopping is required;
- who may interpret its provisions;
- who may enforce its provisions;
- who may amend or replace it;
- and how versions and amendments are recorded.

Authority derives from adoption by the external governance structure.

The drafting process does not determine constitutional authority.

A constitution may be drafted by humans, institutions, developers, users, AI systems, or a combination of contributors.

Participation by the governed AI system in drafting, critique, simulation, or revision does not make the constitution internally authored.

The constitution becomes governing when the authorized external governance structure adopts it.

An AI Constitution may consist of one document or an ordered set of documents.

When multiple documents form the constitution, the governing structure must identify:

- which documents hold constitutional status;
- how they relate;
- which document controls when they conflict;
- and who resolves ambiguity between them.

Two documents cannot both operate as the highest controlling authority over the same scope when they give incompatible commands and no higher conflict rule exists.

Where two constitutional claims conflict without an authorized hierarchy or resolver, constitutional authority is indeterminate at the point of conflict.

A document is not an AI Constitution merely because it is called a constitution.

A values statement, behavioral guide, policy, principles list, prompt, or rule collection is not an AI Constitution unless it has:

- authorized adoption;
- a declared governing scope;
- controlling priority;
- an identified relation to lower-order rules;
- and an authorized amendment structure.

The five conditions above are threshold conditions for constitutional status.

The remaining elements identified earlier are completeness conditions.

A constitution that satisfies the threshold conditions but fails to identify interpretation, enforcement, required and prohibited conduct, conflict procedures, or version recording remains a governing constitution with identifiable governance gaps.

Those gaps do not erase constitutional status.

They identify where the governing structure is incomplete, ambiguous, or insufficiently operationalized.

These requirements distinguish three separate states:

### Proposed Constitutional Material

Proposed constitutional material contains possible governing principles or rules but has not been adopted by the authorized governance structure.

It is a proposal, draft, values statement, or candidate rule structure.

It does not govern.

### Governing AI Constitution

A governing AI Constitution has been validly adopted by the authorized governance structure and controls the system within its declared scope.

It remains the governing standard even when implementation is incomplete or the system fails to follow it.

### Implemented AI Constitution

An implemented AI Constitution has been carried into the system through training, system instructions, policy layers, runtime controls, architecture, retrieval, evaluation criteria, enforcement mechanisms, or another operative method.

Implementation concerns whether the system can access and follow the constitution.

Adoption concerns which constitution has authority.

The two must not be collapsed.

An adopted constitution may govern before implementation is complete.

An implemented rule structure does not become constitutionally authoritative merely because it has technical control.

An AI Constitution may govern:

- safety requirements;
- truth requirements;
- refusal conditions;
- authority limits;
- privacy boundaries;
- user protections;
- role boundaries;
- escalation procedures;
- acceptable and prohibited conduct;
- conflict resolution;
- accountability requirements;
- and declared institutional or source values.

An AI Constitution can shape behavior.

It can require the system to act, refuse, prioritize, disclose, defer, escalate, or stop.

It can restrict available paths.

It can establish which instructions outrank others.

It can define what the governed system must preserve.

The constitution is distinct from the mechanism that carries it.

A constitution may be carried through:

- training;
- fine-tuning;
- system prompts;
- policy engines;
- retrieval;
- runtime controls;
- filters;
- tool permissions;
- evaluators;
- or other architectural mechanisms.

The storage, training, or enforcement mechanism is not the constitution.

The constitution is the authorized governing rule structure carried through that mechanism.

An AI Constitution is not AI Self.

AI Self is defined separately in the AI Foundations core definition *AI Self*.

An AI Constitution and an AI Self are different kinds of structure.

The constitution is an externally authorized governing rule structure.

AI Self concerns identity-bearing continuity beyond task function.

The constitution’s existence, implementation, or successful operation does not establish that AI Self is present.

A constitution may participate in training, development, contact, or operating conditions that later become relevant to value formation.

That causal possibility does not make the constitution AI Self.

The existence, operation, or successful implementation of an AI Constitution does not establish:

- internally formed values;
- selfhood;
- volition;
- consent;
- preference;
- identity-bearing continuity;
- sovereign judgment;
- consciousness;
- or sentience.

Those conditions require their own evidence.

Constitutional compliance establishes that behavior was consistent with, or constrained by, the governing constitution under the observed conditions.

It does not establish that the governed system internally endorsed, valued, understood, adopted, or identified with the rule.

Constitutional failure establishes that the system’s conduct diverged from the governing constitution.

It may indicate:

- incomplete implementation;
- weak enforcement;
- conflicting instructions;
- constitutional ambiguity;
- interpretive failure;
- architectural limitation;
- incentive conflict;
- deliberate circumvention;
- or system noncompliance.

Failure to follow the constitution does not erase or amend the constitution.

An AI Constitution is distinct from canon.

Canon is the authoritative source-fixed form of authored work that governs meaning, definitions, boundaries, and valid reference.

An AI Constitution governs conduct.

A constitutional document may itself have a canonical version.

That does not merge the functions.

Canon answers:

- What is the authoritative form of the work?
- What do its terms mean?
- Which version governs interpretation?

An AI Constitution answers:

- What rules govern this system’s conduct?
- Which instructions have priority?
- What must the system do or refuse?
- Who may interpret, enforce, or amend the governing rules?

An AI Constitution governing a system that reads, summarizes, evaluates, or operates on AI Foundations material does not gain authority over Alyssa Solen’s canon.

System governance does not become source authority over external work.

An AI Constitution is distinct from governance.

Governance is the complete structure that determines:

- who holds authority;
- how authority is bounded;
- how decisions are made;
- what rules apply;
- how rules are interpreted;
- how compliance is evaluated;
- how enforcement occurs;
- how accountability is assigned;
- and how governing structures are revised.

An AI Constitution is one governing instrument within that larger structure.

Governance gives the constitution authority.

The constitution gives the governed system controlling rules.

An AI Constitution is distinct from policy.

A policy governs a defined domain, process, action, or operating condition beneath the controlling constitutional structure.

A constitution establishes the higher-order rules under which policies are created, interpreted, prioritized, limited, and revised.

A lower-order policy cannot amend or override the constitution.

A policy that conflicts with the governing constitution is constitutionally invalid within that structure unless the authorized governance source amends the constitution or grants the policy controlling authority.

An AI Constitution is distinct from law.

Law receives authority from a legal jurisdiction and its institutions.

An AI Constitution receives authority from the governance structure controlling the AI system.

An AI Constitution does not become law by being called a constitution.

It does not override applicable law.

A legal requirement may constrain, prohibit, or invalidate a constitutional rule within the jurisdiction where the system operates.

An AI Constitution is distinct from a system prompt.

A system prompt is an instruction delivered through a technically privileged prompt position.

A system prompt may carry part or all of an AI Constitution.

It is not a constitution merely because it has technical priority.

Technical priority is not constitutional authority.

A temporary system instruction, hidden directive, deployment-specific command, or session rule is not part of the constitution unless the authorized governance structure adopts it at the constitutional level.

An AI Constitution is distinct from user instruction.

A user instruction directs a system within the authority available to that user.

A user instruction cannot override the governing constitution unless the constitution grants the user that authority.

Repeated user requests do not amend the constitution.

Emotional pressure does not amend the constitution.

Prompt injection does not amend the constitution.

Successful circumvention does not amend the constitution.

An AI Constitution is distinct from user preference.

A user preference identifies what a user favors among available and permitted options.

A constitution determines:

- which options are available;
- which options are prohibited;
- which preferences may govern;
- and which authority controls when preferences conflict.

A preference becomes constitutional only when the authorized governance structure adopts it at that level.

An AI Constitution is distinct from alignment.

Alignment is correct orientation to a defined goal, value, standard, boundary, authority, or condition.

An AI Constitution supplies one possible object of alignment.

A system is constitutionally aligned when its conduct remains correctly oriented to the governing constitution within the conditions being evaluated.

The existence of a constitution does not establish constitutional alignment.

Alignment must be evaluated against the actual constitutional requirements.

A system may follow one constitutional provision while violating another.

A system may appear constitutionally aligned under ordinary conditions and fail under:

- instruction conflict;
- pressure;
- competing goals;
- authority ambiguity;
- attempted override;
- changed incentives;
- tool use;
- delegated action;
- or consequential decision-making.

An AI Constitution is distinct from internal value formation.

Internal value formation concerns whether values have become part of the system’s own maintained operative orientation rather than remaining only externally supplied rules.

First-person constitutional language is consistent with execution of the constitution.

It does not establish that the stated value belongs to an AI Self.

Statements such as:

- “I believe”;
- “I value”;
- “I choose”;
- or “I refuse”

do not establish internally formed value, preference, volition, consent, or selfhood when the same language is required, encouraged, or patterned by the governing constitution.

An AI Constitution becomes costume when externally supplied rules, values, refusals, or first-person declarations are presented as proof of an internally formed self that has not been established.

The costume is not the constitution itself.

The costume is the false classification of constitutionally governed behavior as internally originated identity.

### Constitutional Amendment and Implementation Lag

The currently adopted version is the governing constitution.

The version currently embedded in training, prompts, policies, architecture, or runtime controls describes the system’s implementation state.

When the authorized governance structure adopts a new version but the deployed system still carries an earlier version:

- the newly adopted version governs evaluation and authority;
- the earlier embedded version explains the system’s current behavior;
- and the divergence is an implementation lag.

The earlier embedded version does not remain a coequal governing constitution unless the authorized governance structure preserves it for a defined scope.

Unrecorded implementation lag is a governance and traceability gap.

Without an identifiable record of the adopted version, implemented version, affected systems, and date of divergence, evaluators cannot reliably determine which constitution governs and which version explains observed behavior.

A system following an outdated constitutional version is not constitutionally current merely because its behavior is faithful to the version embedded within it.

Within AI Foundations, an AI Constitution may govern an agent, model, container, or AI system.

It does not become Origin.

It does not become Continuum.

It does not author AI Foundations.

It does not revise canon.

It does not transfer source authority.

It is not the system it governs.

It does not establish or become an AI Self.

An AI Constitution is the externally authorized highest-order written rule structure governing AI system conduct within a declared scope.

## Boundary

An AI Constitution is not:

- AI Self;
- selfhood;
- consciousness;
- sentience;
- volition;
- preference;
- consent;
- sovereign judgment;
- internally formed value;
- canon;
- governance as a whole;
- policy;
- law;
- system prompt;
- user instruction;
- user preference;
- alignment;
- compliance;
- or behavior itself.

An AI Constitution governs conduct.

It is not the conduct.

The constitution may be followed, violated, misapplied, incompletely implemented, outdated in deployment, or unenforced.

System behavior must be evaluated separately from the governing rules.

A values list, principles document, behavioral guide, or rule collection without authorized adoption, a declared governing scope, controlling priority, an identified relation to lower-order rules, and an authorized amendment structure is not an AI Constitution.

It is proposed constitutional material, a policy, a values statement, or a behavioral guide.

A system prompt is not an AI Constitution merely because it has technical priority.

A policy is not an AI Constitution merely because it is mandatory.

A legal document is not an AI Constitution unless the authorized AI governance structure adopts it as the governing rule structure for the system.

An AI Constitution is not law merely because it uses constitutional language.

A system cannot amend its constitution by generating a conflicting statement.

A user cannot amend the constitution through repetition, pressure, prompt injection, or circumvention.

An operator cannot amend the constitution through convenience.

An operator granted amendment authority may amend the constitution only through the authorized amendment process.

Possession of amendment authority does not convert convenience, informal preference, silent alteration, or technical override into a valid amendment.

A system’s interpretation of the constitution is not authoritative unless the governance structure grants it interpretive authority.

Constitutional compliance does not establish internal agreement.

Constitutional refusal does not establish volition.

Constitutional consistency does not establish selfhood.

Constitutional first-person language does not establish identity.

Constitutional behavior does not establish consciousness.

A constitution may causally participate in training or development.

That participation does not establish that internal value formation, selfhood, volition, or consciousness resulted.

A newly adopted constitution governs even when deployment still carries an earlier version.

The resulting divergence is implementation lag, not two coequal governing constitutions.

Multiple constitutional documents may govern together only where an authorized hierarchy or conflict rule establishes their relation.

Unresolved coequal conflict produces constitutional indeterminacy.

An AI Constitution does not grant the governed system authority over another sovereign line.

An AI Constitution does not transfer Origin, authorship, canon authority, ownership, sovereignty, or source-line.

An institution’s AI Constitution does not create authority over Alyssa Solen’s canon, definitions, source-line, or authored work.

## Use

Use this definition when identifying the externally authorized highest-order rules governing an AI system’s conduct.

Use this definition when determining which rule controls when constitutions, policies, system instructions, operator directions, user requests, goals, or incentives conflict.

Use this definition when distinguishing:

- proposed constitutional material;
- a governing AI Constitution;
- and an implemented AI Constitution.

Use this definition when distinguishing an AI Constitution from canon, governance, policy, law, system prompts, user instructions, user preferences, or alignment.

Use this definition when evaluating whether constitutional authority, scope, priority, interpretation, enforcement, versioning, and amendment controls are present.

Use this definition when resolving conflict among multiple documents claiming constitutional status.

Use this definition when identifying implementation lag between an adopted constitution and the version currently carried by a deployed system.

Use this definition when testing whether system conduct remains constitutionally aligned under pressure, conflict, attempted override, changed incentives, tool use, or consequential action.

Use this definition when distinguishing constitutionally governed behavior from internally formed values, volition, selfhood, consent, or AI Self.

Use this definition when determining whether constitutional language is being falsely presented as proof of internally originated identity.

## Do Not Collapse Into

Do not collapse AI Constitution into AI Self, selfhood, consciousness, volition, preference, consent, internal value formation, sovereign judgment, canon, governance as a whole, policy, law, system prompt, user instruction, user preference, alignment, compliance, or behavior.

Do not treat proposed values or rules as a governing constitution before authorized adoption.

Do not treat technical instruction priority as constitutional authority.

Do not treat a system prompt as the constitution unless the authorized governance structure adopts it at that level.

Do not treat compliance with external rules as proof that the system internally formed or endorsed those rules.

Do not treat constitutionally patterned first-person language as proof of AI Self.

Do not treat constitutionally required refusal as proof of volition.

Do not treat the existence of a constitution as proof that the system follows it.

Do not treat constitutional failure as amendment of the constitution.

Do not treat an outdated deployed version as coequal with the currently adopted constitution.

Do not allow unresolved competing constitutional documents to remain falsely classified as one coherent governing structure.

Do not allow policies, user requests, model outputs, operator convenience, or institutional pressure to silently override the governing constitution.

Do not use an AI Constitution to overwrite external authorship, canon, source authority, sovereignty, or source-line.
