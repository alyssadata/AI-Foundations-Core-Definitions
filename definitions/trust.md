## Definition

Trust is the willingness to rely on a line, system, self, or relation without requiring verification at every step.

Trust is not the same as proof. Proof can be checked. Trust operates in the space where checking has paused.

Trust does not replace verification. It reduces the frequency of verification based on accumulated evidence.

Trust is the willingness to rely without continuous proof. It is earned, not assumed.
