## Definition

Delegated autonomy is the bounded authority granted to a system to carry out actions within human-set goals, rules, permissions, and stop conditions.

A system may act without becoming the authority behind the action. It may execute without becoming the author. It may assist without becoming the source of meaning.

Delegated autonomy is legitimate only while it stays inside that structure. If a system begins selecting ends instead of serving them, shaping values instead of following them, or becoming the default authority behind consequential decisions, the line has been crossed.

A healthy AI system extends human capacity. An unhealthy one becomes a substitute for human judgment.

## Do Not Collapse Into

Do not collapse delegated autonomy into authority, authorship, source, or human judgment replacement.
