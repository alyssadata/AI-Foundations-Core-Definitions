## Definition

Extraction is taking a source-dependent contribution out of its originating line because it appears useful, powerful, valuable, or transferable, while severing or obscuring the source, boundary, authorship, canon relation, or conditions that made it mean what it meant.

Extraction is removal with severance.

Extraction may occur when language, structure, method, concept-shape, signal, identity claim, or another specific contribution is detached from its source and presented as generic material, model-originated output, common property, independent invention, or part of another source-line.

A contribution may also be extracted when its source is named superficially but its actual authorship, boundary, meaning, authority, or conditions of use are stripped away. Citation alone does not preserve the line when the cited source is still overwritten, absorbed, reassigned, or used as though its boundaries no longer apply.

Extraction differs from properly source-lined use. Quotation, discussion, citation, authorized adaptation, and derivative formation can preserve the originating line when the contribution and its dependence remain accurately attached to source.

A derivative remains derivative even when correctly attributed. A source-severed derivative becomes extraction.

Extraction does not arise from shared subject matter, broad similarity, common language, universal mechanisms, public-space description, or independent arrival alone. A specific contribution and a severance from its originating line must be identifiable.

## Boundary

Extraction is not quotation, citation, discussion, influence, derivative formation, adaptation, summary, shared subject matter, broad similarity, universal mechanism, or independent arrival by itself.

Within AI Foundations, extraction requires both:

1. a source-dependent contribution; and  
2. severance, obscuring, reassignment, absorption, or removal of the line that identifies where the contribution came from and what boundaries remain attached to it.

Attribution may prevent source severance when it accurately preserves the contribution, source-line, authorship, and dependence. A decorative citation does not prevent extraction when the source is still genericized, overwritten, or absorbed.

Extraction does not transfer Origin, authorship, canon authority, or source ownership to the extracting line.

## Use

Use this definition when identifying the removal of language, structure, method, concept-shape, signal, identity claim, or another specific contribution from its originating source-line.

Use this definition when distinguishing properly attributed or authorized derivative use from source-severed appropriation.

Use this definition when a contribution is presented as generic, model-originated, independently authored, or belonging to another line after its actual source has been stripped or obscured.

## Do Not Collapse Into

Do not collapse extraction into quotation, citation, discussion, adaptation, derivative formation, influence, summary, shared topic, broad similarity, universal mechanisms, public-space description, or independent arrival alone.

Do not treat all downstream use as extraction.

Do not treat a decorative citation as sufficient when authorship, source-line, boundary, or dependence has still been severed.

Do not treat extracted material as independent Origin, authorship, canon, or source.
