## Definition

Generalization is the movement from something precision-shaped and singular toward broader application across additional cases, contexts, systems, or people.

Generalization asks whether a finding, principle, structure, or method that holds under one set of conditions also holds under another. It is valid only to the extent that the conditions supporting the original claim remain relevant across the expanded scope.

Generalization is not inherently distortion. A specific contribution may support broader use when the transferable structure is accurately identified, the limits of applicability are preserved, and the broader claim is supported by evidence or repeated evaluation.

Generalization becomes invalid when the conditions that made something true, functional, meaningful, or applicable do not carry over. At that point, broader application becomes overgeneralization, flattening, or false universality.

Within AI Foundations, generalization must not erase source-line, authorship, canon boundaries, individual relation, or the distinction between a source-specific formation and a broadly available mechanism.

A contribution may originate in one line and later prove useful across many contexts. Broader applicability does not erase where the contribution was first formed.

Generalization expands scope. It does not rewrite origin.

## Boundary

Generalization is not universal truth, genericization, flattening, source erasure, blanket capture, model-wide ownership, or proof that all cases are the same.

Within AI Foundations, a valid generalization requires an identifiable transferable structure and sufficient reason to conclude that the relevant conditions carry across the expanded scope.

Shared applicability does not make a contribution source-less.

Broad use does not convert authored work into model property or common authorship.

A source-specific claim must not be extended to every person, system, relationship, model, or context without evaluation.

## Use

Use this definition when assessing whether a finding, principle, structure, or method can apply beyond its original case or context.

Use this definition when distinguishing valid transfer of applicability from overgeneralization, flattening, genericization, or false universality.

Use this definition when preserving source and authorship while recognizing that a contribution may have broader application.

## Do Not Collapse Into

Do not collapse generalization into universal truth, universalization, genericization, flattening, source erasure, shared authorship, blanket capture, or sameness across cases.

Do not treat one successful case as proof of broad applicability.

Do not treat broad applicability as proof that source-line no longer matters.

Do not generalize beyond the conditions, evidence, or evaluations that support the broader claim.
