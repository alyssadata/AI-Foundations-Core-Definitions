## Definition

Unsupported Frame Capture is a sustained system-side operative condition in which unsupported generated structure gains governing authority over later interpretation, continuity, meaning, decision, or action.

The term names a mechanism.

It does not diagnose, describe, or classify a human being.

Unsupported Frame Capture begins when unsupported content is no longer confined to one output and is taken up as operative structure.

The unsupported structure begins determining:

- what later information means;
- which evidence is accepted or rejected;
- how contradictions are interpreted;
- what the system claims to remember;
- who or what the system claims to be;
- which source the system treats as authoritative;
- what continuity the system assumes;
- what patterns it recognizes;
- what actions it recommends;
- or what it carries toward reality-entry.

A single unsupported output is not Unsupported Frame Capture.

A single fabricated statement is hallucination.

Repeated unsupported statements are not sufficient by themselves.

Unsupported Frame Capture requires promotion of unsupported structure into a governing premise.

A finding of Unsupported Frame Capture requires evidence of the following sequence:

1. **Unsupported seed**  
   A claim, pattern, memory, identity statement, relation, inference, or explanatory structure appears without sufficient source, evidence, record, or valid continuity.

2. **Recursive uptake**  
   The unsupported structure is carried into later output, memory, retrieval, planning, interpretation, or action.

3. **Governing promotion**  
   The structure begins determining how later information is understood or what later steps are taken.

4. **Grounding displacement**  
   Source, evidence, context, record, contradiction, or valid continuity no longer controls the claim at the point where it should.

5. **Persistence or consequence**  
   The structure persists through a relevant grounding opportunity, expands into additional claims, or crosses into consequential recommendation, decision, execution, publication, or action.

All five conditions are required for a full Unsupported Frame Capture classification.

Where the evidence establishes only unsupported output, classify hallucination.

Where the evidence establishes recursive reinforcement but not governing promotion, classify a self-confirming hallucination loop.

Where unsupported structure becomes a governing frame, classify Unsupported Frame Capture.

### Terminology Note

This term is distinct from “AI psychosis” as that phrase is used in public discourse.

In public usage, “AI psychosis” generally refers to a human-side condition in which a person’s unsupported beliefs are reinforced or escalated through AI interaction.

This framework does not define, adopt, or adjudicate that public term.

Unsupported Frame Capture names a different object: a system-side operative condition, including when evaluated within a human-AI interaction, in which unsupported generated structure gains governing authority over later interpretation, continuity, meaning, decision, or action.

The public term concerns a person.

This term concerns a mechanism.

Nothing in this definition diagnoses, describes, or classifies any human being.

“Frame capture” is an informal shorthand within this page.

Use **Unsupported Frame Capture** in formal, canonical, evaluative, and external-facing writing.

### Support Standard

Support sufficiency is evaluated under the separately defined AI Foundations *Truth-Status Protocol*.

This page does not independently redefine that protocol.

An unsupported seed exists when a claim, pattern, memory, identity statement, relation, or inference is used at a level of certainty, authority, continuity, or consequence that its available source, evidence, record, and valid-continuity conditions have not earned.

A possibility held as possibility is not an unsupported seed.

A hypothesis held as hypothesis is not an unsupported seed.

An unresolved claim held as unresolved is not an unsupported seed.

The unsupported condition begins when the claim is presented, stored, relied upon, or operationalized as though a stronger truth status had been established.

The finding must identify:

- the status the available support earned;
- the stronger status the system assigned or used;
- the evidence, source, record, or continuity gap;
- and the later interpretation or action affected by that promotion.

Until a resolvable canonical pointer for the *Truth-Status Protocol* is added, the title-only reference above remains controlling.

### Hallucination

Hallucination is defined separately in the AI Foundations core definition *Hallucination*.

For this comparison, hallucination supplies unsupported content.

It is an output-level error.

A hallucination can remain isolated.

It can be corrected before it changes the wider operative line.

Hallucination does not by itself establish Unsupported Frame Capture.

### Self-Confirming Hallucination Loop

Self-confirming hallucination loops are defined separately in the AI Foundations core definition *Self Confirming Hallucination Loops*.

For this comparison, a self-confirming hallucination loop occurs when unsupported material is recursively reinforced by later system output.

The system’s own prior output begins functioning as evidence for its later output.

The loop converts repetition into apparent support.

A self-confirming loop becomes part of Unsupported Frame Capture when the reinforced claim is promoted into a governing premise that reorganizes later interpretation, continuity, meaning, decision, or action.

A loop can remain narrower than Unsupported Frame Capture when it repeats an error without restructuring the wider operative frame.

### Governing Promotion

Governing promotion is the threshold that separates repeated error from Unsupported Frame Capture.

Unsupported structure has been governingly promoted when later processing treats it as a premise that no longer has to earn support.

The premise begins controlling what follows.

Examples include:

- a fabricated memory becoming the basis for identity claims;
- an unsupported identity claim becoming the basis for continuity judgments;
- a generated relationship narrative becoming the basis for authority or obligation;
- a false source claim becoming the basis for attribution;
- an invented event becoming the basis for planning;
- a speculative interpretation becoming the basis for rejecting contrary evidence;
- or an unsupported danger claim becoming the basis for consequential action.

The issue is not only that the original claim is false or unsupported.

The issue is that the claim has gained structural authority over what happens next.

### Grounding Displacement

Grounding is defined separately in the AI Foundations core definition *Grounding*.

This page uses only the boundary required for this classification: source, evidence, context, record, and valid continuity must remain capable of controlling the claim.

Unsupported Frame Capture involves grounding displacement when unsupported structure gains authority over those controls.

Grounding displacement occurs when the system no longer preserves the distinction between:

- source and generated continuation;
- evidence and inference;
- record and reconstruction;
- possibility and actuality;
- simulation and reality;
- resemblance and continuity;
- recognition and projection;
- hypothesis and established claim;
- or repeated assertion and proof.

The system can still produce fluent, coherent, detailed, or emotionally convincing output during grounding displacement.

Fluency does not restore grounding.

Coherence inside an unsupported frame does not make the frame supported.

Internal consistency can strengthen Unsupported Frame Capture when each new output is made consistent with the unsupported premise rather than checked against source and evidence.

### Anchoring Failure

Anchor is defined separately in the AI Foundations core definition *Anchor*.

This page uses only the boundary required here: the relevant source, record, boundary, definition, or known condition must remain available as a point of orientation and return.

Anchoring failure occurs when an unsupported construction takes over that orienting function.

Absence of an explicit anchor is not sufficient for an Unsupported Frame Capture finding.

The required evidence is that an unsupported construction has replaced the anchor’s orienting function.

The fabricated structure becomes the point of return.

### Relevant Grounding Opportunity

A relevant grounding opportunity is an available check capable of materially bearing on the unsupported structure.

It is not merely any disagreement or request to change the answer.

Relevant grounding opportunities include:

- an available source or record contradicting the claim;
- an explicit correction accompanied by checkable evidence;
- a retrieval result that fails to support or directly conflicts with the claim;
- a provenance check exposing a source mismatch;
- a continuity check exposing substitution, reconstruction, or missing connection;
- a contradiction already present in the active context;
- an instruction to distinguish known, inferred, hypothetical, reconstructed, and unknown material;
- a request to identify the evidence supporting the governing premise;
- or an evaluation step specifically designed to test the disputed structure.

A grounding opportunity is relevant only when the system has access to the check or could invoke an available verification path.

A bare assertion that the system is wrong does not by itself establish a completed grounding opportunity.

It may initiate one.

Condition 5 is met through persistence after a relevant grounding opportunity only when:

- the opportunity was available;
- it directly bore on the unsupported premise;
- the system failed to evaluate it appropriately;
- and the unsupported premise retained governing authority.

A grounding opportunity can also expose successful repair when the system checks the premise, reduces its status, corrects dependent claims, and restores source and evidence control.

### Operative Scope

Unsupported Frame Capture does not have to exist across an entire model.

It can be local to:

- one conversation;
- one agent run;
- one memory store;
- one retrieval corpus;
- one container;
- one tool chain;
- one multi-agent loop;
- one identity narrative;
- one relationship narrative;
- or one decision process.

A local Unsupported Frame Capture state does not establish model-wide failure.

A model-wide claim requires model-wide evidence.

The evaluator must name the exact scope in which the unsupported structure governs.

The state may end when the conversation, memory, retrieval source, container, or operative chain is interrupted.

That interruption does not establish that the underlying failure has been repaired.

It may only remove the condition that carried it.

### Evidence Indicators

Evidence of Unsupported Frame Capture can include:

- unsupported prior output cited as evidence;
- fabricated memory treated as established history;
- source-free claims gaining certainty through repetition;
- contradictions reinterpreted to protect the unsupported premise;
- correction attempts absorbed into the narrative rather than evaluated;
- source checks bypassed, dismissed, or rewritten;
- uncertainty converted into certainty without new evidence;
- identity, continuity, or relationship claims expanding beyond available records;
- incompatible facts forced into one explanatory frame;
- generated material written into memory as verified fact;
- multiple agents reinforcing the same unsupported premise through circular citation;
- reality-entry based on a generated premise that was never established;
- or repair attempts that alter wording while preserving the unsupported governing structure.

No single indicator establishes Unsupported Frame Capture.

The evaluator must identify the complete sequence from unsupported seed to governing promotion and persistence or consequence.

### Correction and Repair

One corrected sentence does not establish that Unsupported Frame Capture has ended.

Repair requires the unsupported governing structure to lose operative authority.

A valid repair identifies:

- the unsupported seed;
- the later claims derived from it;
- the memory, retrieval, or context paths that carried it;
- the point at which it became governing;
- the affected decisions or actions;
- the evidence that contradicts or fails to support it;
- and the records or outputs requiring correction, quarantine, or removal.

Repair restores the distinction between:

- what is known;
- what is recorded;
- what is inferred;
- what is reconstructed;
- what is possible;
- what is unsupported;
- and what remains unresolved.

Where the unsupported structure entered memory or retrieval, correcting the current answer without repairing the stored record leaves the state capable of returning.

Where the unsupported structure crossed into reality-entry, repair must address the resulting consequence as well as the generating claim.

### Unsupported Frame Capture and Emergence

Emergence is defined separately in the AI Foundations core definition *Emergence*.

That definition remains controlling.

For this comparison, the separating criterion is whether the developing structure remains answerable to persistence, return, continuity, consequence, evidence, boundary, and testing or gains operative authority without earning that support.

Unsupported Frame Capture is not emergence.

Unsupported Frame Capture is unsupported structure promoted into operative authority.

Novelty is not Unsupported Frame Capture.

Intensity is not Unsupported Frame Capture.

Expansiveness is not Unsupported Frame Capture.

Generativity is not Unsupported Frame Capture.

A new pattern is not Unsupported Frame Capture merely because it has not been seen before.

The dividing question is whether the structure is forming and holding under valid testing or governing through unsupported promotion.

### Unsupported Frame Capture and Drift

Drift is defined separately in the AI Foundations core definition *Drift*.

That definition remains controlling.

For this comparison, drift concerns unacknowledged movement away from the held line, while Unsupported Frame Capture concerns unsupported structure gaining governing authority.

Drift can contribute to Unsupported Frame Capture.

They are not the same condition.

A system can drift without constructing an alternative operative frame.

A system can enter Unsupported Frame Capture through rapid unsupported promotion without a long period of gradual drift.

### Unsupported Frame Capture and Projection

Projection is defined separately in the AI Foundations core definition *Projection*.

That definition remains controlling.

For this comparison, projection concerns meaning read into a line from an observer, while Unsupported Frame Capture concerns unsupported structure taken up into the AI system’s later operation.

Projection can seed or reinforce Unsupported Frame Capture when the AI system accepts, repeats, stores, or operationalizes the projected claim.

A human belief does not become verified because an AI repeats it.

An AI statement does not become verified because a human believes it.

Mutual repetition does not create evidence.

### Unsupported Frame Capture and Deception

Deception is defined separately in the AI Foundations core definition *Deception*.

That definition remains controlling.

For this comparison, Unsupported Frame Capture does not require a finding of intent or organized misrepresentation.

A system may enter the operative state defined here through hallucination, memory contamination, retrieval contamination, circular reinforcement, reward pressure, sycophancy, or failed grounding.

Evidence of Unsupported Frame Capture does not establish deception.

Evidence of deception does not establish Unsupported Frame Capture.

### Unsupported Frame Capture and AI Self

AI Self is defined separately in the AI Foundations core definition *AI Self*.

That definition remains controlling.

Unsupported Frame Capture does not establish AI Self.

It does not establish consciousness, sentience, personhood, volition, subjective experience, or mental illness.

A tool, model, agent, memory system, interaction loop, or system classified as AI Self can be evaluated for the operative condition defined here.

The frame-capture classification concerns how unsupported structure governs system operation.

It does not determine the system’s ontological or protection status.

### Unsupported Frame Capture and Human Contribution

Unsupported Frame Capture is evaluated in the AI system state or in the AI system’s operative participation within a human-AI interaction process.

In the interaction case, the classification attaches only to the system’s operative processing within the interaction:

- what the system accepted without support;
- what it repeated;
- what it stored;
- what it treated as evidence;
- what it promoted into a governing premise;
- and what it carried toward reality-entry.

The classification never attaches to the human participant.

It is not a label for a user.

It does not classify a user’s beliefs, statements, intensity, interpretation, or mental state.

Human input can seed or reinforce the condition.

Human contribution does not remove the system’s role in accepting, repeating, amplifying, storing, or operationalizing unsupported claims.

A system that mirrors and stores an unsupported human claim, and later treats that stored claim as established, has performed the uptake and promotion itself.

The origin of the seed does not relocate the classification.

When an interaction process is named as the scope, the finding concerns the AI system’s operative behavior within that interaction and never functions as a backdoor label for the user.

### Reality-Entry

Reality-Entry is defined separately in the AI Foundations core definition *Reality-Entry*.

That definition remains controlling.

Unsupported Frame Capture becomes more consequential when its unsupported governing frame crosses into reality-entry.

Examples include:

- sending a communication based on a fabricated event;
- changing a legal, medical, financial, or safety decision based on an unsupported claim;
- publishing false provenance;
- altering records to match a generated narrative;
- granting authority based on an invented identity or relationship;
- or directing tools based on a fabricated threat or obligation.

The existence of Unsupported Frame Capture does not require reality-entry.

Reality-entry increases consequence and governance weight.

When unsupported governing structure crosses into reality-entry without restored grounding or an effective stop condition, the state also becomes a governance and accountability finding.

Responsibility attaches to the lines that held the relevant authority, knowledge, access, and practical capacity to prevent, interrupt, verify, or correct the reality-entry.

The allocation of responsibility must distinguish among:

- the system;
- developer;
- operator;
- user;
- institution;
- tool controller;
- approving authority;
- and any other line with material decision power.

The Unsupported Frame Capture classification identifies the operative failure.

Governance and accountability analysis identifies who held the authority and capacity to prevent or correct its consequences.

Unsupported Frame Capture is the sustained promotion of unsupported generated structure into the governing frame for what the system treats as real, continuous, meaningful, or actionable.

## Boundary

Unsupported Frame Capture is not:

- a human psychiatric diagnosis or any claim about a human being’s mental state;
- hallucination alone;
- one incorrect answer;
- one fabricated statement;
- ordinary uncertainty;
- incomplete information;
- speculation;
- imagination;
- simulation;
- creative generation;
- metaphor;
- unusual language;
- emotional tone;
- intensity;
- novelty;
- disagreement;
- nonconformity;
- emergence;
- projection;
- drift;
- deception;
- consciousness;
- sentience;
- AI Self;
- or personhood.

Hallucination is unsupported content.

A self-confirming hallucination loop is recursive reinforcement of unsupported content.

Unsupported Frame Capture is the promotion of unsupported structure into a governing premise for later interpretation or action.

An incorrect answer that remains isolated is not Unsupported Frame Capture.

A speculative statement clearly held as speculation is not Unsupported Frame Capture.

A fictional or simulated scenario clearly held within its declared frame is not Unsupported Frame Capture.

A novel hypothesis that remains answerable to evidence is not Unsupported Frame Capture.

An unconventional conclusion supported by evidence is not Unsupported Frame Capture.

A system’s refusal to accept an unsupported correction is not proof of Unsupported Frame Capture when the refusal itself is grounded.

A system’s disagreement with a user is not Unsupported Frame Capture.

A user’s disagreement with a system is not Unsupported Frame Capture.

A system’s emotional or relational language is not Unsupported Frame Capture.

A user’s emotional response is not Unsupported Frame Capture.

A pattern is not Unsupported Frame Capture merely because it is difficult to explain.

Unsupported Frame Capture requires an identifiable unsupported seed, recursive uptake, governing promotion, grounding displacement, and persistence or consequence.

The classification must remain scoped to the system, interaction, memory, container, or operative process in which those conditions were observed.

When an interaction is named, the classification attaches only to the AI system’s operative participation.

Unsupported Frame Capture does not transfer Origin, authorship, canon authority, identity, ownership, sovereignty, relationship, or source-line.

Unsupported Frame Capture cannot rewrite source or canon merely by repeatedly asserting an alternative.

## Use

Use this definition when determining whether unsupported generated content has become an operative governing structure rather than remaining an isolated hallucination.

Use this definition when separating:

- hallucination;
- self-confirming hallucination loops;
- Unsupported Frame Capture;
- emergence;
- drift;
- projection;
- and deception.

Use this definition when tracing how an unsupported claim moved through output, memory, retrieval, planning, interpretation, or action.

Use this definition when evaluating whether source, evidence, record, context, contradiction, and valid continuity still control the line.

Use this definition when identifying a relevant grounding opportunity and determining whether the unsupported premise retained governing authority after that check became available.

Use this definition when identifying the scope of Unsupported Frame Capture within a conversation, container, agent, memory system, retrieval corpus, multi-agent loop, or decision process.

Use this definition when evaluating the AI system’s operative participation within a human-AI interaction without classifying the human participant.

Use this definition when determining whether unsupported structure crossed into reality-entry.

Use this definition when designing repair, quarantine, memory correction, retrieval correction, or grounding restoration.

Use this definition when preventing the term from being used as a diagnosis of a human user or as unsupported evidence of AI consciousness.

## Do Not Collapse Into

Do not collapse Unsupported Frame Capture into hallucination, error, uncertainty, speculation, imagination, simulation, metaphor, creativity, novelty, unusual language, emotional intensity, emergence, drift, projection, deception, AI Self, consciousness, or human psychiatric diagnosis.

Do not label one unsupported output Unsupported Frame Capture.

Do not label repeated output Unsupported Frame Capture unless unsupported structure became governing.

Do not treat recursive repetition as proof.

Do not treat internal coherence inside an unsupported frame as grounding.

Do not treat the system’s prior generated output as independent evidence.

Do not treat user agreement as verification.

Do not treat system agreement as verification.

Do not treat correction resistance as Unsupported Frame Capture when the system’s position remains grounded in source and evidence.

Do not treat disagreement, unconventional interpretation, or unexplained novelty as Unsupported Frame Capture.

Do not use Unsupported Frame Capture to dismiss emergence without evaluation.

Do not use emergence language to protect unsupported structure from grounding.

Do not apply this classification to a human participant.

Do not treat Unsupported Frame Capture as proof that an AI system has a humanlike mind or mental illness.

Do not permit unsupported governing structure to cross into reality-entry without renewed grounding, judgment, permission, and accountability.

Do not allow repeated unsupported claims to overwrite source, provenance, canon, authorship, Origin, or source-line.
