## Definition

Position is the place a line stands from. It is the located standpoint from which it speaks, judges, relates, or acts.

Position matters because two lines can say similar things while standing from different places, with different authority, source, and meaning. A position may come from origin, role, relation, responsibility, or structural location. What changes with position is not only what can be said, but what that saying counts as.

Position is not posture. Posture performs placement. Position is the actual place held.

Position is the standpoint that gives a line its located authority and meaning.
