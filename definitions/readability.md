## Definition

Readability is the degree to which a human can actually read, follow, and comprehend a line well enough to judge it rather than merely repeat it.

Readability and legibility are connected, but legibility carries the larger framework load, while readability is narrower and refers specifically to the human level of reading, interpreting, and judging.

## Use

Use this definition when naming human-level ability to read, follow, comprehend, and judge a line.

## Do Not Collapse Into

Do not collapse readability into legibility, repetition, formatting, fluency, or ease of display alone.
