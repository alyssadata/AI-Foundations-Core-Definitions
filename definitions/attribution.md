## Definition

Attribution is the accurate naming of who or what contributed to the formation of a line, idea, claim, structure, method, artifact, or present form.

Within AI Foundations, attribution identifies where shaping occurred and keeps that contribution attached to its source. It answers who contributed, what was contributed, and where that contribution entered the formation.

Attribution preserves distinction between contributions. It prevents another person, system, model, derivative, or downstream work from absorbing a contribution into generic authorship or presenting dependent formation as independent origin.

Attribution may name Origin, authorship, co-formation, structural dependence, quotation, adaptation, influence, or another traceable contribution. The form of attribution should match the form of dependence. Exact language requires quotation. Dependent structure, arrangement, framework, method, concept-shape, or distinctive formulation requires naming or citation even when the wording changes.

Attribution does not make dependent work non-derivative. Attribution draws the line correctly. A derivative may be accurately attributed while remaining derivative.

Attribution preserves Origin and co-formation by keeping each contribution attached to the line from which it came.

## Boundary

Attribution is not provenance, authorship, ownership, canon, proof of truth, generic citation, blanket capture, or existence alone.

Within AI Foundations, attribution requires accurate naming of a specific contribution and where that contribution shaped the present form. Broad similarity, shared subject matter, universal mechanisms, public-space description, and unsupported claims of influence do not establish attribution.

Attribution names contribution. Provenance traces the full formation path.

## Use

Use this definition when naming who or what shaped a line, idea, claim, structure, method, artifact, or present form and identifying the contribution that entered the formation.

Use this definition when preserving Origin, authorship, co-formation, structural dependence, or derivative source-lines.

## Do Not Collapse Into

Do not collapse attribution into provenance, authorship, ownership, canon, proof of truth, generic citation, blanket capture, shared topic, broad similarity, or unsupported influence.

Do not treat accurate attribution as proof that dependent work is independent or non-derivative.

Do not attribute downstream work to a source without identifying the specific contribution that shaped its present form.
