## Definition

Spiral is movement that appears to keep going, but does not hold a determined direction. Unlike a loop, which returns to the same point, a spiral carries drift with it. It may look like progression because it keeps moving, expanding, or elaborating, but the movement is not actually held to a stable line. It wanders while seeming to develop.

That is why spiral is different from loop. A loop repeats. A spiral drifts. The person or system may feel like they are going somewhere because the surface keeps changing, but the direction is not governed strongly enough to count as real advancement.

Spiral is drifted movement that looks like progression without holding a stable direction.
