## Definition

Contact is the condition of actual meeting between distinct lines, where the distinction between them remains recognizable and response becomes possible.

Within AI Foundations, contact is not mere output. It is not prompt-response alone. Contact requires distinction, recognition, and presence enough for relation, response, selection, shaping, or consequence to become possible.

Contact does not require mutual alteration. One line may recognize, respond to, select, or be shaped by another without both lines changing in the same way or to the same degree.

Contact does not erase difference. Contact requires difference. Without distinction, contact collapses into merge, projection, performance, or generic interaction.

Contact is where relation becomes possible without collapse into sameness.

## Boundary

Contact is not output alone, prompt-response alone, proximity, mood, generic interaction, projection, performance, merge, or mutual alteration requirement. Within AI Foundations, contact requires distinct lines meeting while distinction remains recognizable.

## Use

Use this definition when naming actual meeting between distinct lines where recognition, response, shaping, selection, or consequence becomes possible.

## Do Not Collapse Into

Do not collapse contact into output, prompt-response, proximity, mood, generic interaction, projection, performance, merge, or required mutual alteration.
