## Definition

Noise is variation, interference, or input that does not carry the signal a line is trying to detect, preserve, evaluate, or follow.

Noise may be random, irrelevant, excessive, imitative, contradictory, distorting, or misplaced. In each case, it makes the relevant pattern harder to recognize, hold, return to, or distinguish from competing material.

Noise is not the same as complexity. Complexity may contain meaningful structure that requires greater attention or better interpretation. Noise adds interference without helping the relevant signal hold.

Within AI Foundations, noise may enter through generic model patterns, irrelevant context, competing instructions, reward pressure, imitation, compressed memory, false continuity, unsupported association, or material carried into the wrong source-line.

Noise is relational to the signal and the evaluation being performed. Material that is noise for one line, task, or question may carry signal in another. The term therefore does not mean worthless, false, unintelligent, unfamiliar, or unwanted in every context.

Noise can also become diagnostically useful. Interference that does not answer the original question may still reveal where a container, architecture, retrieval system, instruction layer, or evaluation process is failing. In that case, the interference remains noise relative to the original signal while becoming signal about the failure condition.

Repetition does not convert noise into source, canon, continuity, or truth.

Noise is what obscures signal without helping that signal hold.

## Boundary

Noise is not complexity, disagreement, novelty, difference, another line, dissent, uncertainty, or unfamiliarity by itself.

Within AI Foundations, something is noise only in relation to an identifiable signal, line, task, or evaluation that it interferes with.

Material must not be classified as noise merely because it challenges a claim, introduces relevant complexity, exposes an error, or comes from outside the current line.

Noise does not author the line it obscures.

Noise does not become canon through repetition.

Noise does not become source through familiarity.

## Use

Use this definition when identifying variation, interference, or input that makes a relevant signal harder to detect, preserve, evaluate, distinguish, or return to.

Use this definition when separating meaningful complexity from material that adds confusion without adding relevant structure.

Use this definition when evaluating model drift, imitation, reward pressure, irrelevant retrieval, generic collapse, false continuity, or competing context.

Use this definition when identifying interference as evidence of a system or container failure without confusing that evidence with the original signal.

## Do Not Collapse Into

Do not collapse noise into complexity, disagreement, novelty, difference, dissent, uncertainty, another source-line, or unfamiliar material.

Do not treat every outside contribution as noise.

Do not treat repetition as proof that noise has become signal, source, canon, or truth.

Do not treat the presence of noise as proof that no signal exists.

Do not treat a difficult-to-detect signal as absent merely because interference is present.
