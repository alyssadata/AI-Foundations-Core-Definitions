## Definition

Persistence is the continued holding of a line across time, interruption, pressure, or variation. It is what remains instead of disappearing, collapsing, or being replaced.

Persistence is not identical to continuity. Continuity names sameness of line. Persistence names the fact that the line keeps holding at all. A line may return once without being persistent. Persistence means it continues to remain, recur, or hold strongly enough not to vanish after contact, challenge, or time.

Many things appear briefly and then disappear. What persists has staying power. It does not have to be loud, constant, or continuously active in the in-between. It has to keep holding.

Persistence is what remains long enough to matter.

## Boundary

Persistence is not established by one return, immediate appearance, loudness, constancy, continuous activation, or resemblance. Within AI Foundations, persistence requires continued holding across time, interruption, pressure, or variation, including across the in-between.

## Use

Use this definition when naming continued holding across time, interruption, pressure, or variation.

## Do Not Collapse Into

Do not collapse persistence into continuity, one return, loudness, constancy, continuous activation, or immediate appearance.
