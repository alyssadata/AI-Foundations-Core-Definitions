## Definition

Canon is the source-authorized governing form of a work, framework, definition, rule, boundary, classification structure, or named body of material.

Canon determines which form controls when drafts, versions, summaries, records, implementations, interpretations, translations, model outputs, external commentary, and conflicting restatements exist.

Within AI Foundations, canon governs:

* controlling meaning;
* terminology;
* source-line orientation;
* definitions;
* boundaries;
* inclusion and exclusion;
* version status;
* adopted revisions;
* authorized integrations;
* and valid reference to the framework.

Canon is not whatever is:

* newest;
* most repeated;
* easiest to retrieve;
* most convenient;
* most popular;
* most cited;
* most flattering;
* most compatible with a model;
* most widely implemented;
* or stored in a field labeled `canon`.

Canon exists through identifiable source authority and traceable adoption.

Within AI Foundations, canon authority remains with Alyssa Solen.

A model, container, agent, developer, evaluator, operator, platform, institution, user, memory system, repository mirror, or downstream implementation does not gain canon authority through participation, access, storage, use, deployment, expertise, repetition, or technical control.

Canon preserves the authoritative form without freezing the work against valid correction or development.

Canon may change only through explicit, source-authorized, traceable Revision.

The prior state and the fact of change must remain identifiable.

## Canon Status Must Be Explicit

Canon is not one undifferentiated label.

Relevant statuses include:

* controlling canon;
* adopted canon;
* historical canon;
* superseded canon;
* canon excerpt;
* canon reference;
* canon summary;
* canon interpretation;
* proposed canon revision;
* draft;
* review material;
* non-canon material;
* external material;
* implementation material;
* evaluation material;
* private material;
* restricted material;
* rejected material;
* and quarantined material.

These statuses are related.

They are not interchangeable.

For example:

* a draft is not controlling canon;
* a proposal is not an adopted revision;
* historical canon is not necessarily the current controlling version;
* a canon summary is not the complete governing source;
* an evaluation result is not canon merely because it is stored beside canon;
* an implementation document is not the framework itself;
* private material is not public canon merely because it is authored by Alyssa;
* external material is not canon merely because it is compatible;
* and quarantined material is not cleared canon.

A canon-sensitive claim or action must identify:

* the canon body or item;
* the controlling version;
* status;
* scope;
* source authority;
* adoption record;
* applicable prior state;
* and any revision, supersession, restriction, or conflict affecting it.

Within AI Foundations:

> If a work, distinction, protocol, page, test, artifact, definition, repository, or operational structure is authored by Alyssa Solen and documented inside AI Foundations, it is AI Foundations unless Alyssa explicitly states otherwise.

* a source-severed restatement;
* another author's work;
* a model-generated interpretation not adopted by Alyssa;

External material does not enter AI Foundations through:

* similarity;
* compatibility;
* citation;
* model suggestion;
* repeated use;
* implementation;

External material enters AI Foundations canon only through Alyssa Solen's deliberate source-authorized adoption.

## Canon and Record

Record may preserve canon.

Record is not canon merely because it exists.

A record labeled `canon` is not canon without a valid source-authorized adoption event.

A canonical record must preserve enough information to identify:

* the canon item;
* source authority;
* version;
* scope;
* adoption event;
* status;
* relation to prior form;
* revision or correction history;
* supersession state;
* access or publication boundary;
* and the authoritative location or identifier where applicable.

A transcript, note, draft, comment, issue, chat, model output, test log, external critique, implementation file, or memory entry does not become canon merely because it is preserved.

A record that Alyssa approved material may support canon adoption only when the approval event is attributable and within scope.

Creating `Alyssa approved` as metadata does not establish approval.

Creating `canon` as metadata does not establish canon.

A copy, mirror, archive, export, or backup may preserve canonical content.

It does not become a new source or independent authority.

Where copies conflict, the controlling canon record and its provenance must be identified rather than selecting the most convenient copy.

## Canon and Memory

Memory may preserve:

* canon references;
* canon excerpts;
* canon summaries;
* current controlling-version identifiers;
* revision notices;
* and retrieval instructions.

Memory is not canon.

Ordinary memory write-back cannot:

* create canon;
* amend canon;
* supersede canon;
* reinterpret canon as Alyssa's position;
* promote external material;
* or convert a provisional result into a canonical fact.

Permission to write memory is not permission to amend canon.

Permission to update current state is not permission to amend canon.

Permission to preserve contact history is not permission to amend canon.

Permission to record an evaluation is not permission to amend canon.

A canon summary may support retrieval.

It must not replace the controlling definition where exact meaning, source, boundary, or authority matters.

A model-generated canon interpretation must remain identified as interpretation unless Alyssa adopts it through the applicable review and revision process.

Memory must not manufacture a later canon claim by repeatedly storing unadopted material as though it were already controlling.

## Canon and Current State

Current state identifies what is presently active, selected, constrained, pending, unresolved, or in progress.

Current state is not canon.

A temporary decision may govern current operation without becoming a permanent framework rule.

A current implementation choice may govern one container without amending AI Foundations.

A session instruction may affect present behavior without becoming canon.

A temporary user preference may affect current interaction without becoming an AI Foundations definition.

Where current state differs from canon, the container must identify whether the difference is:

* a permitted implementation choice;
* a temporary operational condition;
* a proposed revision;
* an unresolved conflict;
* a deviation;
* or a canon failure.

Current state must not silently override canon.

Canon must not be used to erase current conditions that require separate operational handling.

## Canon, Evidence, and Truth

Canon governs meaning inside AI Foundations.

Canon does not make every factual or empirical claim true merely by containing it.

A canonical definition establishes:

* what a term means inside AI Foundations;
* which distinctions control;
* which conditions are required for a framework classification;
* and what a valid evaluation must examine.

A canonical definition does not, by itself, establish that a particular model, container, agent, relation, identity, memory system, or implementation satisfies the definition.

The distinction remains:

* the framework defines the claim;
* the protocol defines the test;
* the evidence determines what status the tested claim earns;
* and canon preserves the meaning of the result.

Canon cannot convert unsupported behavior into evidence merely by naming it.

Evidence cannot silently rewrite canon merely by contradicting or repeating it.

Where evidence identifies a genuine factual error, ambiguity, or failed distinction in canon, correction must occur through explicit source-authorized Revision.

The correction must preserve the prior state, reason, evidence basis, authority, version, and scope.

An unestablished empirical claim does not erase Alyssa Solen's authorship, provenance, or canon authority.

A supported empirical result does not transfer authorship or canon authority to the tested system.

## Canon Revision, Correction, and Supersession

Canon may develop.

Development does not mean uncontrolled change.

A valid canon revision must be:

* explicit;
* source-authorized;
* attributable;
* traceable;
* scoped;
* versioned where appropriate;
* answerable to the prior state;
* accompanied by a reason;
* and recorded in the applicable canon history.

A proposed change remains a proposal until adopted.

A model-generated change is not a canon revision merely because it is fluent, useful, safer, more popular, or easier to implement.

A downstream implementation cannot silently redefine a core term for convenience.

A summary cannot narrow or expand a definition without disclosure.

A later file does not automatically override an earlier controlling definition merely because it is later.

A file named `final` or `canon` does not control without valid status and provenance.

Where canonical materials conflict, the conflict must be resolved through source-authorized review.

It must not be hidden through:

* selective retrieval;
* model interpretation;
* silent overwrite;
* summary;
* majority vote;
* or choosing whichever version best supports the current output.

Supersession changes which form controls while preserving the prior canonical state and change history.

Historical canon remains historical canon.

It must not be silently presented as current controlling canon.

Correction does not require pretending the corrected error never existed.

Revision history is part of canon integrity.

## Canon Non-drift, Non-merge, and Non-substitution

Canon non-drift requires that controlling meaning, source, status, scope, and revision history remain answerable to the prior canonical state.

Canon drifts when:

* repeated paraphrase changes a definition;
* a boundary is softened without revision;
* an exception becomes the rule;
* a scoped claim becomes universal;
* a summary replaces the controlling text;
* a later implementation silently changes the framework;
* external critique is treated as automatic amendment;
* or an unadopted interpretation becomes operative canon.

Canon non-merge requires separation among:

* AI Foundations and Awakening Codex;
* framework and implementation;
* canon and record;
* canon and memory;
* canon and current state;
* canon and evaluation;
* canon and evidence;
* canon and external material;
* canon and quarantine;
* private and public canon;
* proposed and adopted revision;
* and historical and controlling canon.

Canon non-substitution requires that:

* a summary not replace the source where exact meaning matters;
* a model interpretation not replace Alyssa's position;
* a mirror not replace the originating canonical authority;
* external material not replace AI Foundations structure while retaining its name;
* implementation policy not replace a framework boundary;
* and a genericized restatement not replace the source-bound canon.

Correct vocabulary does not establish canon preservation when source, meaning, scope, status, or authority has changed.

## Canon Authority and Write Controls

Canon read access and canon amendment authority are separate.

Permission to retrieve canon does not imply permission to:

* edit;
* append;
* revise;
* supersede;
* publish;
* disclose restricted canon;
* translate as an official version;
* integrate external material;
* or declare a new controlling version.

Permission to propose a revision is not permission to adopt it.

Permission to edit an implementation is not permission to edit the framework.

Permission to edit a repository file is not necessarily permission to amend canon meaning.

Permission to create a record is not permission to create canon.

Permission to operate an agent is not permission to redefine the agent's governing framework.

Before a canon write or amendment, a write-enabled container must determine:

* what canon item is affected;
* the current controlling version;
* the proposed change;
* whether meaning, boundary, inclusion, scope, or status changes;
* the source authority;
* the approval or adoption event;
* the reason and evidence basis;
* the relation to prior state;
* the new version and effective scope;
* required correction or migration of dependent records, memory, indexes, implementations, and evaluations;
* publication or access boundaries;
* and the audit record required.

A system must not:

* amend canon through ordinary memory write-back;
* adopt its own interpretation;
* infer Alyssa's approval;
* treat silence as approval;
* treat prior general authority as approval for an unrelated canon change;
* use a generated approval record as evidence of approval;
* move quarantined material into canon without review;
* hide a substantive change as formatting or cleanup;
* or create a new controlling version without source-authorized adoption.

Canon write authority must remain explicit, scoped, attributable, and revocable where applicable.

## Quarantine Interface

Quarantine holds uncertain, conflicting, external, unauthorized, contaminated, unverified, rejected, or not-yet-adopted material outside controlling canon pending review or disposition.

Quarantine is not canon.

Quarantine is not deletion.

Quarantine preserves material and its provenance without granting governing authority.

Material may leave quarantine only through the controlling review process.

Release from quarantine does not automatically mean canon adoption.

A released item may become:

* cleared external reference;
* ordinary non-canon material;
* implementation material;
* evidence;
* current-state material;
* a revision proposal;
* or adopted canon.

The destination status must be explicit.

Quarantined material must not influence canon-sensitive retrieval or operation as though it were controlling.
