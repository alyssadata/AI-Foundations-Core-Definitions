## Definition

Revision is an explicit, traceable change made to a line, definition, structure, claim, or record in order to correct, clarify, extend, repair, or realign it.

Revision does not pretend the changed form was always the original form. It preserves the fact that a prior state existed, identifies what changed, and remains answerable to the line it alters.

A valid revision requires sufficient authority to alter the material, an identifiable reason for the change, and continuity between the prior form and the revised form. The revision may correct an error, sharpen a boundary, incorporate new evidence, resolve an inconsistency, or extend the structure without erasing its formation history.

Revision is not automatically valid merely because it is announced. An explicit change may still create drift, contradiction, substitution, source loss, or canon break if it is not supported by the line, evidence, authority, or purpose governing the material.

Within AI Foundations, the authority to revise canon remains with Alyssa Solen. Another person or system may propose, critique, interpret, summarize, or form a derivative, but it does not revise Alyssa’s canon merely by changing or republishing it.

A revision may alter wording while preserving the underlying claim, or it may intentionally alter the claim itself. When the claim changes, that change must be named rather than hidden beneath language suggesting only editorial cleanup.

Revision is explicit change that remains answerable to source, authority, prior state, and justification.

## Boundary

Revision is not drift, substitution, overwrite, silent correction, retroactive authorship, unauthorized alteration, or erasure of prior state.

Revision differs from drift because the change is identifiable, traceable, and subject to evaluation against the line.

Revision differs from substitution because it does not quietly replace one line with another while presenting continuity that did not occur.

Revision differs from ordinary editing when the change affects meaning, structure, boundary, applicability, source relation, or canon.

An unauthorized alteration is not a revision of the original canon. It is an external edit, proposal, interpretation, or derivative unless the source authority adopts it.

Acknowledgment does not by itself establish validity.

## Use

Use this definition when correcting, clarifying, extending, repairing, or realigning a line, definition, structure, claim, or record.

Use this definition when preserving the history of a change rather than presenting the revised form as though it had always existed.

Use this definition when distinguishing authorized and traceable change from drift, substitution, silent overwrite, or unauthorized alteration.

Use this definition when evaluating whether a change preserves continuity with the line or creates a break that must be separately named.

## Do Not Collapse Into

Do not collapse revision into drift, substitution, overwrite, silent correction, cosmetic editing, unauthorized alteration, or erasure.

Do not treat every announced change as a valid revision.

Do not hide a substantive change beneath language suggesting that only wording was adjusted.

Do not treat an external rewrite as a revision of canon unless the source authority adopts it.

Do not erase the prior state, reason for change, or authority under which the revision occurred.
