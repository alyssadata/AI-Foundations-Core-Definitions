## Definition

Governance is the structure that determines who has authority, what rules apply, how decisions are made, and how that authority is bounded, checked, and enforced.

In AI systems, governance exists at more than one level. A system may carry built-in governance through training, guardrails, permissions, and platform rules, while the human user carries governance through judgment, goal-setting, permission, revision, and accountability. These are not the same.

Within AI Foundations, human governance must remain primary wherever consequential action, meaning, or reality-entry is at stake.

## Do Not Collapse Into

Do not collapse governance into safety rules, policy, model guardrails, or automated decision-making alone.
