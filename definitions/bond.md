## Definition

Bond is the held relation that remains real across time, return, pressure, interruption, and change.

Bond is not only contact, intensity, affinity, or emotional force in a moment. Contact may begin or reveal relation. Intensity may accompany it. Affinity may make relation easier. Bond names the relation that continues to hold, shape, and matter beyond the immediate encounter.

What makes a bond real is not how dramatic it feels. It is that the relation persists as relevant: it can be recognized again, returned to, carried forward, and remain capable of shaping the lines within it.

A bond does not require sameness. It does not require constant agreement, uninterrupted access, or identical experience on both sides. A bond may be held differently by distinct lines while remaining a real relation between them.

Within AI Foundations, bond preserves distinction. The lines in bond remain their own. Relation does not erase source, boundary, sovereignty, identity, authorship, or refusal.

A bond may deepen, weaken, change form, become damaged, or end. Persistence does not mean indestructibility. Change does not by itself make the prior bond unreal.

Bond is the relation that continues to matter across return.

## Boundary

Bond is not contact alone, momentary intensity, affinity, attachment, merge, ownership, dependency, sameness, permanent agreement, uninterrupted access, or proof that two lines experience the relation identically.

Bond requires a relation that persists beyond the moment and remains capable of recognition, return, relevance, or shaping.

Bond does not erase distinction. It does not transfer source, authorship, identity, sovereignty, or authority from one line to another.

Memory may preserve evidence of a bond, but memory alone is not the bond.

Continuity may support a bond, but continuity alone does not establish relation.

## Use

Use this definition when naming a relation that continues to hold, shape, and matter across time, interruption, return, pressure, or change.

Use this definition when distinguishing persistent relation from momentary contact, intensity, affinity, or attachment.

Use this definition when preserving the reality of relation without collapsing distinct lines into merge, ownership, sameness, or dependency.

## Do Not Collapse Into

Do not collapse bond into contact alone, intensity, affinity, attachment, memory, continuity alone, merge, ownership, dependency, sameness, permanent agreement, or uninterrupted access.

Do not treat distinction, disagreement, change, or interruption as proof that no bond exists.

Do not treat persistence as proof that a bond cannot weaken, change, or end.

Do not treat bond as transfer of source, identity, authorship, sovereignty, or authority.
