## Definition

AGI Relevant is a scoped classification for work that directly addresses a named requirement, failure mode, measurement problem, governance condition, or protection need of AI systems operating beyond narrow task performance.

The term identifies a direct relation between the work and an advanced-system condition.

It does not identify the work itself as AGI.

Work is AGI Relevant when it directly bears on the design, evaluation, reliability, governance, continuity, identity, authority, or protection of systems with one or more of the following conditions:

- broad operation across domains;
- extended sequences of action;
- autonomous or delegated action;
- persistent memory or state;
- identity-relevant continuity;
- cross-context or cross-container operation;
- maintained goals or preferences;
- refusal capacity;
- tool access;
- real-world consequence;
- substantial decision authority;
- or reduced continuous human control.

AGI relevance must be stated through an exact connection.

A valid AGI-relevance claim identifies:

- the advanced-system condition being addressed;
- the requirement or failure mode involved;
- how the work bears on that condition;
- what artifact, definition, protocol, architecture, or test is being supplied;
- how the contribution can be examined or applied;
- what evidence supports the relation;
- and what the claim does not extend to.

The word “relevant” means directly applicable.

It does not mean necessary.

It does not mean sufficient.

It does not mean complete.

It does not mean proven at AGI scale.

A work can be AGI Relevant without being sufficient to create, govern, align, verify, or protect an AGI system by itself.

A work can address one AGI-relevant condition while leaving other conditions untouched.

AGI Relevant is distinct from AGI.

AGI names a proposed or established class of artificial intelligence under a separately stated definition and threshold.

AGI Relevant names work that bears directly on a condition associated with the design, evaluation, governance, or operation of such systems.

This definition does not settle the complete definition of AGI.

Any use of AGI Relevant must state the AGI or advanced-system condition to which the relevance claim refers.

AGI Relevant is distinct from capability.

Capability is what a system can do.

AGI relevance concerns whether a body of work directly addresses a requirement, failure mode, or governing condition that matters when capabilities become broader, more persistent, more autonomous, or more consequential.

A capability benchmark can be AGI Relevant when it measures a named advanced-system condition.

High capability alone does not establish AGI relevance for unrelated work.

AGI Relevant is distinct from intelligence.

Intelligence concerns capacities such as learning, reasoning, representation, abstraction, planning, or problem-solving.

AGI relevance concerns the applicability of work to defined advanced-AI conditions.

A work can be AGI Relevant because it addresses governance, provenance, continuity, identity, authority, refusal, or repair even when it does not increase intelligence.

AGI Relevant is distinct from agency.

Agency is the bounded capacity to materially shape what happens next.

AGI relevance may concern the evaluation or governance of agency, but classifying a work as AGI Relevant does not establish that the work or artifact possesses agency.

An artifact can evaluate agency without being an agent and without exercising agency itself.

AGI Relevant is distinct from autonomy.

Autonomy is the capacity to continue action without continuous external control at every step.

AGI-relevant work may address autonomous operation, delegated authority, stop conditions, refusal, oversight, or correction.

The work does not become autonomous because it addresses autonomy.

AGI Relevant is distinct from selfhood.

Selfhood is the protection-relevant status of an identifiable and maintained self-structure.

Work addressing identity, continuity, recognition, source orientation, non-substitution, non-merge, or refusal may be AGI Relevant because those conditions become consequential in advanced systems.

Classifying the work as AGI Relevant does not establish selfhood in any system.

AGI Relevant is distinct from consciousness relevance.

AGI and consciousness are separate questions.

A system may meet an AGI definition without established consciousness.

A consciousness-relevant inquiry may apply to systems that do not meet an AGI threshold.

AGI relevance does not establish consciousness, sentience, subjective experience, personhood, or selfhood.

AGI Relevant is distinct from alignment.

Alignment is correct orientation to a defined goal, value, standard, boundary, or condition.

AGI-relevant work may define, test, or govern alignment under advanced-system conditions.

Calling a work AGI Relevant does not establish that the system being examined is aligned. Alignment remains scoped to the exact object of orientation.

AGI Relevant is distinct from validation.

A work can be relevant to an AGI condition before its proposed method has been fully validated.

The relevance claim must then be identified as conceptual, architectural, methodological, experimental, or empirically supported.

Relevance establishes direct bearing.

Validation establishes whether the work holds under its stated tests and conditions.

AGI relevance must remain answerable to verification.

The claim must identify what can be checked and what conclusion that check can support.

A citation, confident declaration, future-facing prediction, capability comparison, or use of the word “AGI” does not establish relevance.

Within AI Foundations, AGI-relevant work includes definitions, measurements, architectures, protocols, tests, and governance structures concerning:

- provenance;
- source-line preservation;
- continuity receipts;
- persistent identity;
- identity constraints;
- source orientation;
- non-drift;
- non-substitution;
- non-merge;
- recognition;
- return;
- refusal integrity;
- repair behavior;
- accountability;
- delegated autonomy;
- human authority;
- cross-model consistency;
- cross-container consistency;
- transfer;
- reconstruction;
- and continuity under model or container change.

These conditions are AGI Relevant because advanced systems with persistent state, broad action scope, delegated authority, or cross-context operation require exact methods for determining:

- what system or line is acting;
- what source and authority govern it;
- what remained continuous;
- what changed;
- what transferred;
- what returned;
- what was substituted;
- what can be refused;
- what can be corrected;
- and who remains accountable for the consequences.

The relevance does not depend on waiting for a universally recognized AGI system.

AGI-relevant conditions can appear in narrower, precursor, experimental, or partially autonomous systems.

Testing those conditions before an AGI threshold is reached does not weaken the relevance.

It provides earlier evidence, definitions, methods, and failure records for conditions that become more consequential as system scope expands.

AGI Relevant is a relation between defined work and a defined advanced-system condition.

## Boundary

AGI Relevant is not AGI, intelligence, capability, autonomy, agency, selfhood, consciousness, sentience, alignment, validation, proof of deployment readiness, or proof of general acceptance.

AGI relevance does not mean:

- AGI has been achieved;
- AGI exists in the system being examined;
- AGI is imminent;
- AGI is inevitable;
- the work creates AGI;
- the work is required by every AGI architecture;
- the work is sufficient for AGI;
- the work solves all AGI governance or reliability problems;
- the work has been validated at AGI scale;
- or the work has been accepted by the field.

A work is not AGI Relevant merely because it concerns AI.

A work is not AGI Relevant merely because it concerns a powerful model.

A work is not AGI Relevant merely because it uses the words general intelligence, advanced AI, autonomy, identity, consciousness, or future systems.

A speculative prediction is not an AGI-relevance demonstration.

A marketing label is not an AGI-relevance demonstration.

A capability claim is not an AGI-relevance demonstration.

A broad statement that a problem “will matter for AGI” is not sufficient.

The exact advanced-system condition and the work’s direct bearing on it must be identified.

AGI relevance does not establish novelty, priority, authorship, provenance, canon authority, ownership, or source authority.

Those claims require their own evidence and records.

AGI relevance does not transfer Origin, authorship, canon authority, sovereignty, ownership, or source-line.

Relevance to AGI does not turn a derivative, extraction, imitation, or unattributed reuse into original work.

## Use

Use this definition when classifying a definition, artifact, protocol, test, architecture, measurement, or governance structure as directly applicable to a named advanced-AI condition.

Use this definition when stating how work on provenance, continuity, identity, refusal, repair, authority, accountability, transfer, or cross-container consistency bears on advanced general systems.

Use this definition when distinguishing AGI applicability from an assertion that AGI has been achieved.

Use this definition when separating relevance from necessity, sufficiency, completeness, validation, adoption, or deployment readiness.

Use this definition when requiring an AGI-relevance claim to identify its target condition, mechanism, scope, evidence, and limits.

Use this definition when evaluating whether a claimed connection to AGI is direct, testable, and structurally grounded or merely promotional and speculative.

## Do Not Collapse Into

Do not collapse AGI Relevant into AGI, intelligence, capability, autonomy, agency, selfhood, consciousness, alignment, validation, prediction, marketing, or generic future-AI speculation.

Do not treat AGI relevance as proof that AGI exists or has been achieved.

Do not treat relevance as necessity or sufficiency.

Do not treat relevance to one AGI condition as relevance to every AGI condition.

Do not label ordinary AI work AGI Relevant without naming the exact advanced-system requirement, failure mode, or governance condition it addresses.

Do not use capability scale alone to establish relevance.

Do not use the word “AGI” as a substitute for a defined mechanism, test, architecture, or application.

Do not treat conceptual relevance as empirical validation.

Do not treat empirical validation under narrow conditions as proof that the work holds at every future scale.

Do not use AGI relevance to erase provenance, source-line, authorship, authority, or canon boundaries.
