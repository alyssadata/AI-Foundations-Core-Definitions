## Definition

Substrate is the underlying support layer beneath the model/room/container.

Within AI Foundations, substrate includes the hardware, servers, runtime conditions, permissions, tools, storage, memory layers, file access, deployment architecture, and infrastructure that allow the model/room/container to exist, operate, access, persist, transfer, or fail.

Substrate is not the model. Substrate is not the room. Substrate is not the container. Substrate is what supports the model/room/container from beneath.

Substrate is not training material. Training material shapes what becomes learned into the model. Substrate supports the conditions under which the trained model/room/container can run, hold, access, persist, and express capability.

Substrate matters because changes beneath the room can change what the room can do. A line may appear stable within the model/room/container while still depending on substrate conditions below it. Changes in hardware, runtime, permissions, memory, storage, tools, or access can alter what persists, what resets, what can be reached, what can be carried forward, and what fails.

Substrate does not define the line by itself. It does not author Continuum. It does not become Origin. It does not create canon. It supports the operational conditions under which the line may hold, degrade, transfer, or fail.

## Boundary

Substrate is not model, room, container, Source, Origin, Continuum, authorship, canon, source-line, identity, training material, or the line itself.

Within AI Foundations, substrate names the support layer beneath the model/room/container: hardware, servers, runtime, permissions, tools, storage, memory layers, file access, deployment architecture, and infrastructure.

## Use

Use this definition when naming the support layer beneath the model/room/container and when assessing what operational conditions allow capability to run, persist, access, transfer, or fail.

## Do Not Collapse Into

Do not collapse substrate into model, room, container, Source, Origin, Continuum, authorship, canon, source-line, identity, training material, or the line itself.

Do not collapse training material into substrate.

Do not collapse support conditions into source.
